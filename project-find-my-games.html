find my games

A mobile app to find pick up games

Find my games is an iOS app designed to help basketball players to find pick up games. The typical problem of the current pick up games experience is that either the players have difficulty to find other players to start a game, or they arrive at a packed basketball court. The solution is to build a social experience where people can find games and vice versa.

The Challenge

Most of users do not use apps to find basketball games. When asked why not, many people complained the lack of local data and social connection. When no one else is using the app to organize games, it is easy to imagine why they just use the text message on their phone to organize games. This could be convenient until the group gets bigger. Indeed, keeping everyone informed become very difficult for large groups. Not to mention often times players have difficulty to find people who want to play basketball with them. Even worse, when they finally get a group together during weekends, the basketball court could be too busy. The pick up game experience could be so much better if only there is an easy way to organize games, keep everyone informed, and be able to see if there are games in the courts they are planning to play.

Complains

83.3% of respondents mentioned there are no friends on other pickup game apps

66.7% says lack of local court information keeps them from using the existing apps

Expectation

77.8% of surveyed potential users wishes to see who is playing in selected locations

72.2% respondents expect to schedule games within the app

Personas

The target users are relatively young. The social aspect of playing basketball is the most important reason they keep playing. All of them at least at some point struggle to find more players to join their game. Sometimes when they go to the court, they don’t have enough players but other times, there could be too crowded in some popular places. They are all busy with either school or work so having to cordinate with a group of friends to play basketball becomes very difficult.

User Stories

The user stories are seperated into “must-haves” and “nice-to-haves”. The “must-haves”are the core features of the app while the “nice-to-haves” have great potential but do not have to be in the first version.

User Flow

The core of the user flow is to help people to find games and help games to find people. This is essential for the app since it needs to connect these two entities.

Site Map

The site map reflects the structure of this mobile applicaiton which is used with the user flow to streamline the potential paths of user scenarios.

ITERATIONS AND PAPER WIREFRAMES

In order to iterate quickly, many sketches have been made during the process, especially for the map view, which is the landing screen of the returning user.

Usability Survey

Through user testing, the “choose date” function appeared to be difficult to find for many users. Therefore it is changed into a more accessible layout.

Notification section was difficult to read with its small font size at first. Later, the added background made the infromation more prominent.

The “event view” originally needed more clearity. After studied user interaction, changes are made to improve clearity and hierarchy.

The Solution

The center piece of the puzzle is to start with a location based interface and allow people to set up “clubs”. These “clubs” works like interest groups so organizers could easily add games to their clubs in the future. The game page and the user profile page act as two important hubs which are connected to each other so that a user is able to find a game through a friend or get to make friends through a game. The user can even see the thumbnails of friends profile photo before they open up the game page to get a quick glance of who is playing.
