A WEB APP THAT LETS YOU
SHARE, SAVE AND COLLABORATE

Blocbox is a web app that allows people to store notes, websites, and images. The landing page allows new users to understand the main features of the website and encourages users to sign up with social network accounts. The focus of the web app UI is easy-of-use since it helps users to keep, find, and organize their content.

Personas

The user personas cover a wide range of scenarios from day to day life to productivity process. It is not wise to try to solve all of their problems at once. However the ease of use and “handiness” became the obvious goal of the design.

“I often wish when i write something down, I could set a reminder to come back to it later”

Julie benafits from note-taking and bookmarking apps in her everyday live. She uses note taking app to help her to remember small everyday things and school notes that are not worth bring out a notebook. She only uses bookmark functions with the browser bookmark bar so she could always find what she saved.

“I like it when I can paste something into my phone app and it appears on my computer immedietely”

Mark uses note-keeping apps mostly for personal purposes. He likes it that the note app syncs with his laptop so that he can quickly copy and paste paragraphs in to the note app and get it from the computer side. He also like the convenience of the app that comes with the phone but he does have one little complain about the phone app does not organize the notes very well.

"If something is very important to me, I would try to remember it; but if it is not, it is ok to forget"

Nick has a very good memory so he remembers most things and does not feel the need to write them down most of the time. However he uses note taking app some times in order to get work related things more organized. He also use Microsoft Word for longer notes but he finds it too complex to use.

User Flow

The new user flow is optimized for discovery and the return user flow is catered for efficiency. Being able to add new content and access stored content quickly become the major goal.

Site Map

The site map shows how features are organized for ease of access. Content managing, content editing and collaboration are prioritized for immediate access when returning users log in.

LOW-FIDELITY WIREFRAMES

Many itterations of low-fidelity wireframes are made to explore different ideas of the homepage layout. Eventually, different parts of each itteration got picked, combined and iterated further.

HIGH-FIDELITY WIREFRAMES

After many versions of itteration, the low-fidelity wireframes are developed with more detailes to consider and compare, which was also used for early usability testings.

LANDING PAGE IMPROVEMENT

More iterations are made base on different details applied during the refining process. The design of the hero elements send strong branding messages and communicate unique advantages of this product: simplicity, tangiblity, and efficiency.

USABILITY TESTING

During the process of usability test, attention to detail and empathy play a major role in eliminating user frustrations. Sometimes it means asking the right questions, but other times the designer can gain important insights merely by observing the users interaction with the prototype.

Usability survey shows user prefers later positioning of the “Add” button which is more proximate to other major user interactions when navigating content.

Since the background fits the height of the browser perfectly, it causes navigational issues while surveyed users interacting with the content. Therefore a symbol was created to guide users to scroll down.
